Challenges

Keeping Up with Rapidly Changing Technologies:

Technology evolves at an incredible pace. New programming languages, frameworks, and tools emerge constantly, and staying updated requires continuous learning. Sometimes, it feels overwhelming to keep up, but I’ve learned to embrace the challenge by staying curious and adaptable.

Debugging and Problem-Solving:

One of the most frustrating yet rewarding aspects of coding is debugging. I’ve spent sleepless nights trying to figure out why my code isn’t working, only to realize that a tiny syntax error was the problem. However, these struggles have strengthened my analytical thinking and patience.

Imposter Syndrome:

At times, I’ve doubted my abilities, especially when surrounded by peers who seem more experienced. But through perseverance and continuous learning, I’ve realized that growth comes with time, effort, and persistence.

Balancing Studies and Personal Projects

Pursuing an Honours degree while working on personal and academic projects can be overwhelming. Managing time effectively has been a skill I’ve had to develop, ensuring I balance studies, coding, and personal life.
